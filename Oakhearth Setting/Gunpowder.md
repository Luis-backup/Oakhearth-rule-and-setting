Gunpowder has been a known material for a couple of centuries at this point, however it has not really achieved wide spread use for a variety of regions. 

Firstly, sourcing both sulfur and saltpeter in high quantities and of high quality is incredibly expensive. An effect of this expense is that the actual design of the firearms has not really grown, leaving them unwieldly and inaccurate. Compared to crossbows they are
- slow
- expensive
- difficult to repair
- inaccurate


Secondly, it has a poor reputation, the few who have experience with gun powder is actually through Goblins. Once a goblin colony grows large enough, they will start to produce Goblin Alchemist who occasionally create firearms for their clan. The weapons are crude and ugly with most breaking after a few uses. However, the limited amount of study that has been conducted has show that the gunpowder goblins produce (often referred to as Goblin Powder) tends to be more powerful than those made by people. Regardless, the association with Goblins has dissuaded many potential artisans from developing the weapons, often seeing them as a cursed or unlucky craft.

Thirdly of course is magic. Those in need of powerful ranged weaponry often turn to the use of wands powered by enchanted jewel. The wands most often use fire magic because striking a fire jewel generates a spark which the magic then uses to shoot a burst of flam from the end. These types of wands are called Firebows. This name comes from the shape that these wands take, being based on crossbows, the barrel helping to guide and focus the magic. 
Because fire is the most common, this type of design, regardless of the actual element is often referred to as Firebows.

- The nature of the enchanted jewels means that they can be infinetrly recharged as long as they remain undamaged. While not cheap by any means (cost of the jewel plus labor of the Jewel Carver), their reliability and ease of use makes them popular among those whose are wealth enough to purchase them

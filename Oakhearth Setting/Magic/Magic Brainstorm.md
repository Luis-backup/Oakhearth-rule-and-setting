Magician = cast spells or performs rituals

Mana is an innate part of every living thing. It is the energy of cycles with Life and Death being the cycle that most greatly creates Mana.
A living thing's mana connects its soul to the world around it. This is what allows for spells to be cast, the user shaping their mana to affect themselves or the world around them. However, this connection goes both ways. If a person exists near or exposed to a strong source of magic, that energy will flow back into the living thing. This results in the many of the Uncommon Ancestries (tieflings, assimar, ect)and Sorcerers

As a person becomes older and as they become stronger, they will naturally be able to contain more mana within themselves.  For magicians, this results in them being able to cast stronger spells. For non-magician characters, the increased mana allows them to perform (from a reader's perspective) superhuman feats.
A person can expend more mana than their body can normally handle, but this often results in recoil that can cause short or long term damage of varying severity

Mana connects to the soul and the soul connects to the body. Therefore, the body has a direct connection to the magic a magician uses. This results in magic being reflected within the body of the magician
Ex:
- Someone who often cast fire magic will be warmer to the touch than a normal person and their finger tips may be permanently singed or stained with ash
- A draconic sorcerer may start with a few scales covering a bit of their back or scar, but once they become powerful, they may have scales covering their entire arm and shoulders.

# Types of Magic
Scholars have roughly defined three types of magic in the word: Ancient, Aether, and Ordered. Although many argue that Ancient and Miracle should be one category or that Miracles should be a subtype of Ancient.

## Arcane
Arcane magic is ordered magic. Spellcasting relies on the creation of sigils and speaking magic words.

More powerful spells require more sigils to cast and longer phrases of magic words

Rituals done with Arcane magic are logically consistent. For example creating a familiar requires you to use actual animal meat that it reshapes to create a familiar

Creating rituals and sigils is dangerous work and often destructive so a lot of research focuses on modifying existing spells or researching forgotten spells.

Magic words: So from a real-world perspective, the magic words are actually Latin written in reverse so a basic fire spell would singi (ignis in reverse). In setting, the magic words are derived from the Draconic language since Arcane magic was originally invented by the dragons (all dragons are evil/greedy) to dominate mortals and fight the Giants.

Sigils: From a real-world perspective, the spell sigils are based on real world chemistry .
- The basic fire sigil reflects the real world recipe for black powder: the outermost circle that makes holds the rest of the circle represents carbon. Within the circle there is an octagon (sulfur) with circles at its points that tangentially touch the circle at each of its points. Within that octagon is another circle and within the inner most circle there is a upside-down y with a circle at each of its ends and at the bend (potassium nitrate)
- Basic Ice spells look similar to snowflakes. the outermost  shape is an hexagon with circles at each point (two tightly connected H20). Within that octagon there is a ring of 6 tightly connected H20 molecules. the final shape is an inner most hexagon that represent more water molecules

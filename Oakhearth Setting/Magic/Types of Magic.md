Scholars have roughly defined three types of magic in the word: Ancient, Aether, and Ordered. Although many argue that Ancient and Miracle should be one category or that Miracles should be a subtype of Ancient.

## Ordered
 Ordered magic is the best understood form type of magic. It is the type of magic that uses sigils. Ordered magic is tied to the physical world and follows some understandable logic. Because of this consistency, Wizards and other scholars are actually able to study and develop these types of spells, combining existing knowledge an sigils to create new spells. 

The actual reason that sigils work is unknown. Scholars generally agree that the are shaped Mana, but no one can seem to find an actual explanation as to why certain shapes produce their effects.
To better understand this conundrum, take lightning and thunder. Thunder always follows lightning, but no one knows why, we just know that it will occur. Of course, different religions each have an explanation as to why it occurs, but the fact that none of them agree, means that we don't actually know. This is how sigils work. We know certain shapes produce specific effects, but we don't know why.

In Gaultism, the stories say that sigils are the alphabet or notes of creation, and that Gault used the symbols to form the world.
